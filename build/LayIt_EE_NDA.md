## 1. PURPOSE

The Owner is developing a proprietary hardware product (the "LayIt Laser" tile installation laser projection system) and wishes to engage the Contractor to provide electrical engineering services including schematic review, PCB design, prototype assembly, and testing. This Agreement protects confidential information shared between the Parties during this engagement.

---

## 2. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

"Confidential Information" means any and all non-public information disclosed by either Party to the other, whether orally, in writing, electronically, or by inspection, including but not limited to:

(a) Hardware designs, schematics, circuit descriptions, PCB layouts, and component specifications;

(b) Firmware source code, algorithms, and communication protocols;

(c) Software application source code, user interface designs, and data formats;

(d) Patent applications, invention disclosures, and intellectual property filings (whether pending or contemplated);

(e) Bills of materials, sourcing information, and supplier relationships;

(f) Product specifications, feature descriptions, and roadmaps;

(g) Business plans, pricing strategies, marketing plans, and financial information;

(h) Any materials marked "Confidential," "Proprietary," or with similar designation; and

(i) Any information that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure.

---

## 3. EXCLUSIONS

Confidential Information does not include information that:

(a) Was publicly known at the time of disclosure through no fault of the Receiving Party;

(b) Becomes publicly known after disclosure through no fault of the Receiving Party;

(c) Was already in the Receiving Party's possession prior to disclosure, as documented by written records;

(d) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information; or

(e) Is disclosed to the Receiving Party by a third party who has the legal right to make such disclosure.

---

## 4. OBLIGATIONS OF THE RECEIVING PARTY

The Receiving Party agrees to:

(a) Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence;

(b) Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party;

(c) Use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing engineering services related to the LayIt Laser project as directed by the Owner;

(d) Limit access to Confidential Information to those employees or subcontractors who have a need to know and who are bound by obligations of confidentiality at least as protective as those contained herein;

(e) Not reverse engineer, decompile, or disassemble any prototypes, samples, or hardware provided by the Disclosing Party, except as necessary to perform the agreed-upon engineering services;

(f) Not file any patent application or other intellectual property registration based on or derived from the Confidential Information; and

(g) Take reasonable precautions to prevent unauthorized disclosure, including but not limited to securing physical documents, password-protecting electronic files, and not discussing Confidential Information in public settings.

---

## 5. OWNERSHIP OF INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

(a) All Confidential Information remains the sole property of the Disclosing Party.

(b) Any inventions, designs, improvements, or works of authorship created by the Contractor in the course of performing services under this Agreement that are derived from or related to the Owner's Confidential Information shall be the sole and exclusive property of the Owner.

(c) The Contractor hereby assigns to the Owner all right, title, and interest in any such inventions, designs, improvements, or works, and agrees to execute any documents necessary to perfect such assignment.

(d) The Contractor retains ownership of their pre-existing tools, methodologies, and general engineering knowledge that are not derived from the Owner's Confidential Information.

---

## 6. RETURN OF MATERIALS

Upon termination of this Agreement or upon request by the Disclosing Party, the Receiving Party shall promptly:

(a) Return all physical documents, prototypes, samples, and materials containing Confidential Information;

(b) Delete or destroy all electronic copies of Confidential Information in their possession; and

(c) Provide written confirmation of such return or destruction within ten (10) business days.

---

## 7. TERM AND TERMINATION

(a) This Agreement shall remain in effect for a period of three (3) years from the date first written above.

(b) The obligations of confidentiality shall survive termination of this Agreement for an additional period of two (2) years, for a total of five (5) years of protection from the date of disclosure.

(c) Either Party may terminate this Agreement at any time with thirty (30) days written notice to the other Party, provided that termination shall not affect the obligations of confidentiality with respect to Confidential Information already disclosed.

---

## 8. NO LICENSE OR WARRANTY

(a) Nothing in this Agreement grants the Receiving Party any license or right to use the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information except as expressly stated herein.

(b) All Confidential Information is provided "as is" without warranty of any kind.

(c) This Agreement does not obligate either Party to enter into any further agreement or business relationship.

---

## 9. REMEDIES

The Parties acknowledge that unauthorized disclosure of Confidential Information may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages may be inadequate. The Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity.
