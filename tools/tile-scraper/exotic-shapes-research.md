# Exotic & Specialty Tile Shapes — Comprehensive Market Research

Research date: 2026-03-19
Source: Industry knowledge from major retailers (Home Depot, Lowe's, TileBar, Daltile, MSI, Bedrosians, Floor & Decor, Wayfair, BuildDirect, specialty importers)

> This document covers ALL tile shapes found in the real retail market beyond
> the standard shapes already supported in LayIt:
> square, rectangle, hexagon, penny round, fishscale/fan, diamond, octagon+dot,
> arabesque/lantern, picket/elongated hex, herringbone pattern, chevron pattern.

## SHAPES ALREADY SUPPORTED (reference only)

These are listed for completeness — LayIt already renders these:

1. **Square** — 1x1 through 24x24

9. **Picket / Elongated Hex** — stretched hexagon, pointed ends
10. **Herringbone** — rectangular tiles in V pattern (layout pattern, not shape)
11. **Chevron** — parallelogram with angled ends for V pattern

## NEW SHAPES TO ADD

### 1. Ogee / Provenzal / Elongated Arabesque

**Alternate names:** Provenzal, Provencal, Ogee Drop, Teardrop Arabesque, Gothic Arch

**Geometric description:**
- 4-sided shape with TWO convex curved sides (top) meeting at a point, and TWO concave curved sides (bottom) meeting at a wider point
- Think of a pointed Gothic arch shape — wider at top with a pointed tip at bottom
- Vertically elongated compared to standard arabesque
- Approximately 2:1 height-to-width ratio

**Tessellation:**
- Alternating rows, rotated 180° every other tile
- Tiles interlock with concave sides nesting into convex sides of adjacent row
- Offset by half-width each row (like brick offset)

**Typical dimensions:**
- Sub-tile: ~3.5" x 6.5", or ~4" x 8"
- Some larger format: 6" x 12"

**Sheet size:** 10" x 12" or 12" x 12" mesh-mounted (mosaic), also available as individual tiles

**Where sold:**
- TileBar (very popular there)
- Bedrosians
- Floor & Decor
- Wayfair
- MSI Surfaces
- NOT commonly at Home Depot or Lowe's (specialty shape)

**Popularity:** MODERATE — Very trendy 2022-2025 for kitchen backsplashes. Growing rapidly.

### 2. Star and Cross / Star + Cross

**Alternate names:** Star & Cross, Salem Star, Islamic Star, 8-Point Star, Cross & Star

**Geometric description:**
- TWO sub-tile shapes working together:
  - **Star**: 8-pointed star (octagram) formed by two overlapping squares
  - **Cross**: Small cross/plus-shaped filler piece between stars
- The star is an 8-pointed polygon with 16 edges (8 concave notches)
- The cross is a small plus sign with 12 edges

**Tessellation:**
- Stars placed in a grid, crosses fill the gaps
- Repeating unit is one star + one cross
- Straight grid alignment (no offset)

**Typical dimensions:**
- Star: ~4" x 4" or 6" x 6" across points
- Cross: ~2" x 2" or 3" x 3"
- Also available as single large-format tiles with the pattern printed/embossed (not true mosaic)

**Sheet size:** 12" x 12" mesh-mounted

**Where sold:**
- Daltile (Restore line)
- Home Depot (limited selection)
- TileBar
- Bedrosians
- Floor & Decor
- Specialty Moroccan/Islamic tile importers (Zia Tile, Clé Tile, Tabarka Studio)

**Popularity:** MODERATE — Classic pattern, enduring demand. Common in Mediterranean/Moroccan-inspired designs.

### 3. Kite / Dart (Penrose-style)

**Alternate names:** Kite tile, Dart tile, Penrose tile, Quadrilateral kite

**Geometric description:**
- **Kite**: 4-sided polygon, two pairs of adjacent sides equal. Like a geometric kite shape — wider at top, pointed at bottom. Two short sides at top, two long sides tapering to a point.
- **Dart**: The concave complement — looks like an arrowhead / bowtie notch

**Tessellation:**
- Kites alone: radial patterns around a center point (like a pinwheel)
- Kite + Dart: Penrose tiling (aperiodic, non-repeating) — mostly theoretical for real tile
- In practice, kites are sold solo and arranged in simple radial or linear patterns

**Typical dimensions:**
- Sub-tile: ~2" x 4" or 3" x 6"
- Individual field tiles: up to 6" x 12"

**Sheet size:** 11" x 12" mesh-mounted (mosaic versions)

**Where sold:**
- TileBar
- Bedrosians
- Clé Tile
- Walker Zanger
- NOT at Home Depot / Lowe's

**Popularity:** NICHE — Design-forward, seen in high-end projects. Growing interest.

### 4. Rhombus / Parallelogram / 3D Cube

**Alternate names:** Rhombus, Diamond (60°/120°), Parallelogram, Escher Cube, 3D Cube, Tumbling Blocks

**Geometric description:**
- Parallelogram with 60° and 120° angles (NOT a 45° diamond/rotated square)
- All four sides equal length
- When three rhombi of different shades meet at a point, they create a 3D cube optical illusion

**Tessellation:**
- Three tiles meet at each vertex — light, medium, dark shades create cube illusion
- Can also tile in simple rows like bricks
- 60° offset rows

**Typical dimensions:**
- Sub-tile: ~1.5" x 2.5", ~2" x 3.5", or ~3" x 5"
- Large format: 6" x 10"

**Sheet size:** 9" x 10.5" or 12" x 12" mesh-mounted

**Where sold:**
- TileBar (very popular — "Dimension" collection)
- Bedrosians
- Floor & Decor (Merola brand)
- Home Depot (Merola Tile 3D Cube — yes, actually sold at HD)
- Wayfair
- MSI

**Popularity:** COMMON — The 3D cube illusion is a popular trend. Sold at big-box stores.

### 5. Trapezoid / Trapezoidal

**Alternate names:** Trapezoid, Tapered, Wedge tile

**Geometric description:**
- 4-sided polygon with one pair of parallel sides (top shorter than bottom)
- Two non-parallel sides of equal length (isosceles trapezoid)
- Typically about 60-70% as wide at the top as at the bottom

**Tessellation:**
- Alternating orientation (flip every other tile) to create a band/strip
- Can create fan/radial patterns when arranged around a center point
- Rows of alternating up/down trapezoids

**Typical dimensions:**
- Sub-tile: ~2" top x 3" bottom x 3" tall
- Individual tiles: up to 4" x 6"

**Sheet size:** 10" x 12" mesh-mounted

**Where sold:**
- TileBar
- Bedrosians
- Clé Tile
- Walker Zanger
- Specialty retailers only

**Popularity:** NICHE — Uncommon but exists in premium lines. Used for radial patterns around drains or focal points.

### 7. Moroccan Fish Scales / Zellige Scallop

**Alternate names:** Zellige, Zellige Fish Scale, Moroccan Scallop, Cle Zellige, Handmade Scallop

**Geometric description:**
- Similar to standard fishscale but with slight irregularities (handmade)
- Semi-circular top with flattened or slightly pointed bottom
- Key difference from machine-made fishscale: uneven edges, varied thickness, imperfect glaze
- Some Zellige "scales" are more shield-shaped (straight sides with curved top)

**Tessellation:**
- Same as fishscale — offset rows, alternating up
- Irregular edges mean grout lines are thicker/more visible

**Typical dimensions:**
- Individual tile: ~2" x 2" to ~4" x 4"
- Zellige is almost always sold as INDIVIDUAL TILES, not mesh-mounted sheets

**Sheet size:** N/A — sold as loose individual tiles by the sq ft

**Where sold:**
- Clé Tile (primary US source, ~$25-55/sq ft)
- Zia Tile
- Tabarka Studio
- Mosaic House
- Wayfair (imported)
- NOT at Home Depot / Lowe's

**Popularity:** VERY TRENDY — Zellige is one of the hottest tile trends 2023-2026. Primarily backsplash. Expensive.

### 8. Basketweave (as a mosaic pattern shape)

**Alternate names:** Basketweave, Basket Weave, Woven Pattern

**Geometric description:**
- NOT a unique tile shape — uses standard rectangles
- BUT sold as pre-mounted mosaic sheets with the pattern built in
- Pattern: pairs of rectangles alternate horizontal/vertical, creating a woven look
- Often uses two sizes: 1x2 rectangles with a small square dot at intersections

**Tessellation:**
- 2 horizontal rectangles next to 2 vertical rectangles, repeating
- Small square accent at the corners (optional — "basketweave with dot")
- Grid-aligned, no offset

**Typical dimensions:**
- Rectangle sub-tile: 1" x 2" (most common), 2" x 4"
- Dot (if present): 1" x 1" or matching to rectangle width

**Sheet size:** 12" x 12" mesh-mounted

**Where sold:**
- Home Depot (Daltile, MSI, Jeffrey Court)
- Lowe's
- Floor & Decor
- TileBar
- Very widely available

**Popularity:** VERY COMMON — Classic bathroom floor pattern. Sold everywhere. One of the most popular mosaic patterns.

**Note for LayIt:** This is a PATTERN made of rectangles, not a unique shape. Could be rendered as a special layout mode for rectangles.

### 9. Pinwheel / Hopscotch

**Alternate names:** Pinwheel, Hopscotch, Windmill (sometimes)

**Geometric description:**
- NOT a unique tile shape — uses TWO sizes of square tiles
- Pattern: one large square surrounded by four smaller rectangles or squares, creating a spinning/pinwheel effect
- Large square rotated 45° with four small tiles filling the gaps

**Tessellation:**
- Repeating unit: 1 large square + 4 small rectangles arranged around it
- Creates a spinning visual effect
- Grid-aligned overall

**Typical dimensions:**
- Large square: 2" x 2" or 4" x 4"
- Small rectangle: 1" x 2" or 2" x 4"
- Also done with 12" + 6" field tiles

**Sheet size:** 12" x 12" mesh-mounted (mosaic version)

**Where sold:**
- Home Depot (Daltile, Merola)
- Lowe's
- Floor & Decor
- TileBar

**Popularity:** COMMON — Classic pattern, widely available as mosaic sheets.

**Note for LayIt:** Pattern made of standard shapes, not unique geometry. Could be a layout mode.

### 10. Windmill / Spinning Square

**Alternate names:** Windmill, Spinner, Spinning Squares

**Geometric description:**
- Similar to pinwheel but distinct: 4 rectangles around a center square, all at 90° rotations
- Creates a windmill/spinner visual
- Center square is same size as the short side of the rectangle

**Tessellation:**
- Repeating module: 1 center square + 4 rectangles spinning around it
- Modules tile in a straight grid

**Typical dimensions:**
- Rectangle: 1" x 2", 2" x 4"
- Center square: 1" x 1", 2" x 2"

**Sheet size:** 12" x 12" mesh-mounted

**Where sold:** Same as pinwheel — Home Depot, Lowe's, Floor & Decor, TileBar

**Popularity:** COMMON — Often sold alongside basketweave and pinwheel.

**Note for LayIt:** Pattern, not shape. Same as pinwheel note.

### 14. Brick-Joint Hexagon / Offset Hex Pattern

**Alternate names:** Running Bond Hex, Staggered Hex

**Geometric description:**
- Standard hexagon BUT laid in a running bond (brick) pattern instead of the typical hex tessellation
- Same tile shape, different layout
- Creates a different visual rhythm

**Tessellation:** Running bond — each row offset by 1/3 rather than 1/2

**Note for LayIt:** Layout variation, not new shape. Could be a pattern option under hex.

### 15. Cross / Plus Sign

**Alternate names:** Cross, Plus, Swiss Cross, Greek Cross

- 12 edges total (all right angles)

**Tessellation:**
- Crosses interlock with small square filler pieces at the corners

- Grid-aligned

**Typical dimensions:**
- Overall: ~4" x 4" to ~6" x 6"
- Arm width: ~1.5" to 2"

**Sheet size:** Usually individual tiles, not mosaic sheets

**Where sold:**
- TileBar

### 16. Escher / Interlocking / Puzzle Piece

**Alternate names:** Escher, Puzzle, Interlocking, Tessellation tile

**Geometric description:**
- Modified squares/rectangles with notched or curved edges that interlock like puzzle pieces
- Each side has a tab or notch that mates with the adjacent tile
- Multiple proprietary shapes exist (each manufacturer has their own)

**Tessellation:**
- Direct interlock — tiles snap together with no grout gap (or minimal gap)
- Grid-aligned

**Typical dimensions:**
- ~4" x 4" to 8" x 8"

**Where sold:**
- Specialty / artisan tile makers

### 17. Versailles / French Pattern / Roman Pattern

**Alternate names:** Versailles Pattern, French Pattern, Roman Pattern, Ashlar Pattern, Modular Pattern

**Geometric description:**
- NOT a unique shape — uses 4 different sizes of rectangles/squares
- Typically: 8x8, 8x16, 16x16, 16x24 (or similar proportional sets)
- Arranged in a specific repeating layout that looks random but is prescribed

**Tessellation:**
- Specific placement pattern repeated across the floor
- Uses a template/map showing where each size goes
- The repeating unit is typically a large rectangle containing all 4 sizes

**Typical dimensions:**
- Set of 4 sizes, e.g.: 8"x8", 8"x16", 16"x16", 16"x24"
- Also: 6"x6", 6"x12", 12"x12", 12"x18"

**Sheet size:** N/A — individual tiles sold as a matched set

**Where sold:**
- Home Depot (travertine sets)
- Lowe's
- Floor & Decor
- MSI Surfaces (very popular in travertine/stone)
- Daltile

**Popularity:** VERY COMMON — Extremely popular for entryways, patios, kitchens. One of the most-installed patterns for natural stone.

**Note for LayIt:** This is a MULTI-SIZE PATTERN, not a new shape. Major feature request potential — needs a "Versailles pattern" layout mode with 4 tile sizes.

### 19. Moroccan Encaustic / Cement Tile Shapes

**Alternate names:** Encaustic, Cement tile, Hydraulic tile, Cuban tile

**Geometric description:**
- Typically SQUARE tiles (8"x8" most common) but with intricate geometric PATTERNS printed/pressed into the surface
- The tile shape itself is just a square
- Some specialty Moroccan shapes exist as actual non-square cutouts:
  - **Bejmat**: Thin rectangle (~2" x 6"), the Moroccan subway tile
  - **Darj w Ktef** (step & shoulder): stepped/notched rectangle
  - **Zellige square**: small handmade 2"x2" squares with irregular edges

**Tessellation:** Grid (they're squares) — pattern alignment across tiles creates larger motifs

**Typical dimensions:**
- 8" x 8" (most common cement tile)
- Bejmat: 2" x 6"
- Zellige: 2" x 2" individual

**Where sold:**
- Clé Tile, Zia Tile, Mosaic House (Moroccan specialty)
- Cement Tile Shop
- Floor & Decor (growing selection)
- Villa Lagoon Tile
- Granada Tile

**Popularity:** VERY COMMON — Cement tile is a huge trend. The SHAPES are mostly square; the patterns inside are what vary.

**Note for LayIt:** The square shape is already supported. Bejmat is just a thin rectangle. The visual pattern inside the tile is not relevant to layout geometry.

- Usually used as accent tiles within a field of squares or hexagons
- Some designs tile flowers with other shapes to fill gaps

**Typical dimensions:**
- 4" to 8" diameter

**Sheet size:** Waterjet sheets ~12" x 12" with mixed shapes

**Where sold:**
- TileBar (waterjet collections)
- Walker Zanger
- Artistic Tile
- Very high-end / specialty

**Popularity:** RARE — Luxury/designer only. Waterjet = expensive.

### 31. Quatrefoil / Clover / 4-Leaf

**Alternate names:** Quatrefoil, Clover, Four-Leaf, Gothic Quatrefoil

**Geometric description:**
- Four overlapping circles creating a 4-lobed clover shape
- Symmetrical on both axes
- All curves, no straight edges
- Each "petal" is a circular arc

**Tessellation:**
- Tiles in a grid with small pointed filler pieces at the intersections
- The negative space between quatrefoils forms small 4-pointed stars

**Typical dimensions:**
- ~4" x 4" to 6" x 6"

**Sheet size:** Mesh-mounted or individual

**Where sold:**
- TileBar
- Merola Tile (at Home Depot — yes, actually available at HD)
- Walker Zanger
- Wayfair

**Popularity:** MODERATE — Available at big-box stores via Merola. Classic Gothic/Moorish pattern.

### 32. Penny Square / Small Square (micro)

**Alternate names:** Micro Square, Pixel, Mini Square, 5/8" Square

**Geometric description:**
- Very small squares, 5/8" to 1"
- Same as regular square but tiny — always mesh-mounted
- Used for the same visual effect as penny round but with squares

**Tessellation:** Simple grid, no offset

**Typical dimensions:**

### 33. Brick Offset Rectangle (2:1 with specific offset)

### 34. Spearhead / Arrow / Dart

**Alternate names:** Spearhead, Arrow, Dart, Arrowhead

**Geometric description:**
- Arrow/chevron shape but with a notch cut out of the back (concave V at the wide end)
- Like a chevron but hollow at the back
- Creates a more dramatic directional pattern than chevron

**Tessellation:**
- Nests with adjacent tiles — the notch of one receives the point of another
- Creates strong directional flow

**Typical dimensions:**
- Sub-tile: ~2" x 4"

**Sheet size:** 10" x 12" mesh-mounted

**Where sold:**
- TileBar
- Bedrosians

**Popularity:** RARE — Specialty geometric mosaic.

### 35. Scale / Dragon Scale / Pointed Scallop

**Alternate names:** Dragon Scale, Pointed Scale, Gothic Scale, Pointed Fan

**Geometric description:**
- Like fishscale but with a POINTED top instead of rounded
- More angular — the top comes to a point like a Gothic arch
- Straighter sides than fishscale

**Tessellation:**
- Same as fishscale — offset overlapping rows
- Points nest between the tiles in the row below

**Typical dimensions:**
- Sub-tile: ~2" x 2.5" or ~3" x 3.5"

**Sheet size:** 10" x 12" mesh-mounted

**Where sold:**
- TileBar
- Bedrosians
- Wayfair

**Popularity:** NICHE — Alternative to standard fishscale for a more edgy/gothic look.

## RENDERING COMPLEXITY NOTES

**Easy to render (straight edges only):**
- Triangle, Rhombus/3D Cube, Trapezoid, Kite, Half Hex

**Medium complexity (curves involved):**
- Capsule/Paperclip, Quatrefoil, Ogee/Provenzal, Large Scallop, Dragon Scale

**Hard to render (complex curves, multiple sub-shapes):**
- Star & Cross (two shapes), Leaf/Teardrop, Wave, Flower/Daisy, Escher

**Pattern modes (layout logic, not new shape rendering):**
- Basketweave, Pinwheel, Windmill, Versailles — use existing square/rectangle shapes in specific arrangements
