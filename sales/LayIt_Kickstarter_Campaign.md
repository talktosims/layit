## Campaign Description

### The Problem: Layout Is the Hardest Part of Every Tile Job

Cutting tile is mechanical. Grouting is tedious. But layout — figuring out where to start, how to minimize cuts, and how to keep everything straight — that is where skill matters and where most jobs go wrong.

Ask a professional tiler what separates a good job from a bad one and they will say the same thing: it is all in the layout. Start in the wrong place and you end up with 1-inch slivers at the wall. Miscalculate your cuts and you waste $200 in tile. Chalk lines take 30–60 minutes to snap and measure, and one mistake means pulling them up and starting over.

There is no consumer tool that solves this. Tile calculator apps tell you how many boxes to buy — they do not show you where to put them. Laser levels draw one straight line. CAD software costs thousands and requires training. So 65% of DIY backsplash installers and 40% of bathroom floor installers do what everyone has done for decades: they get on their knees with a tape measure and hope for the best.

We built LayIt to fix that.

---

### The Solution: An App That Plans + A Laser That Projects

LayIt is two things:

**1. A free app** that turns your phone into a precision tile layout engine. Enter your room dimensions (or photograph the room and let AI extract them). Scan the tile box label with your camera — AI reads the dimensions, material, shape, and grout spacing. The app generates a pixel-perfect tile layout in seconds, color-coded: green for full tiles, orange for cuts. Tap "Optimize" and it finds the starting position that minimizes waste. Tap any cut tile and it shows you the exact measurement — "Mark at 3-7/8 inches from the left edge."

**2. A laser projector** that beams that layout onto your actual wall or floor at 1:1 scale. Green laser lines show exactly where every tile, every cut, and every grout joint goes. No chalk lines. No dry-laying tiles to check the pattern. No measuring each row individually. The pattern is right there, on the surface, life-size.

This is not a concept render. The app is live and working today with thousands of layouts generated. The laser is a functioning prototype backed by three provisional patents. This campaign funds the first production run.

### How It Works

**The App — 30 Seconds to a Complete Layout**

1. **Measure your space.** Three options: type in width and height for a simple rectangle, tap corners on a canvas for an L-shaped or irregular room, or photograph the room and let AI extract the geometry using monocular depth estimation (no LiDAR required — works on any smartphone).

2. **Identify your tile.** Point your camera at the spec label on the tile box. Our AI (powered by Claude Vision) reads the dimensions, material, shape, and grout joint — even on metric labels, bilingual text, and mosaic sheets. Or scan the UPC barcode for instant lookup from our database of 111+ verified products across 16 brands. Or choose from 234 built-in presets covering every common tile from 2-inch hex mosaics to 8x48 wood-look planks.

3. **See your layout.** The layout renders instantly. Pinch to zoom. Drag to reposition. Tap Optimize and the engine searches for the starting position that minimizes cuts and waste. Add cutouts for outlets, windows, or shower benches — the pattern tiles around them automatically.

4. **Track your cuts.** Lock the pattern. Tap any orange (cut) tile. A full-screen detail view shows the cut line with precise measurements in inches and fractions. Mark each tile as you cut it — it turns red so you never lose your place.

5. **Share and report.** Send the layout as an interactive HTML page to your installer or client. Pro subscribers generate branded 3-page client reports with company logo, layout diagram, and material counts.

**The Laser — Your Layout, Life-Size on the Wall**

The LayIt Laser is a portable projection device that connects to the app over WiFi. It uses a 200mW 520nm green laser steered by dual-axis galvanometer mirrors at 20,000 points per second to draw smooth, flicker-free lines visible in daylight.

What makes it different from a laser level or a toy projector:

- **Vision-based self-correcting alignment (Patent 1).** Point it at a wall corner or a placed tile. The built-in 5MP camera detects edges via computer vision (Canny edge detection, contour extraction, homography). Every tile you place becomes a new reference point — accuracy improves from ±2-3mm at cold start to less than 0.3mm after 25 tiles. No fiducial markers. No manual calibration.

- **Bump recovery.** A 6-axis IMU detects if someone kicks the unit. The camera automatically re-locks to existing tile edges. A green/yellow/red confidence indicator shows alignment status in the app.

- **Absolute positioning.** Errors do not accumulate. Each frame references the actual placed tiles, not the previous projection. This is fundamentally different from how laser levels or CNC machines work.

- **Segmented projection for large surfaces.** A single projection covers a 3-foot work zone. For large floors, the app generates a serpentine path of segments. Complete one zone, move the laser, and it auto-aligns to the tiles you already placed.

## FAQ

**Q: Is the app real, or is this just a concept?**
A: The app is live and working right now. You can download it today and plan a complete tile layout — room dimensions, tile identification, cut optimization, cut tracking. Thousands of layouts have been generated. The laser is what this campaign funds.

**Q: What phones does the app work on?**
A: LayIt works on any modern iPhone (iOS 16+) and as a Progressive Web App (PWA) in any mobile browser. The AI room scanning feature uses Apple Depth Pro and works on any iPhone — no LiDAR sensor required.

**Q: Is the laser safe?**
A: The laser is 200mW Class 3B. It is safe for residential use when following standard precautions: do not look directly into the beam. Every unit ships with OD4+ laser safety glasses. The laser includes hardware safety interlocks and an automatic timeout if no activity is detected. It is comparable in power to commercial laser alignment tools used in construction.

**Q: How accurate is the laser projection?**
A: At cold start (first tile), accuracy is ±2-3mm. As you place tiles, the vision system uses each tile edge as a new reference point. After 25+ placed tiles, accuracy improves to less than 0.3mm (about 1/100 of an inch). Errors do not accumulate — each frame references the actual placed tiles, not the previous projection.

**Q: How big of an area can the laser cover?**
A: A single projection window covers approximately a 3-foot work zone, which is a natural working area for tile installation. For larger surfaces, the app divides the layout into segments. Complete one segment, move the laser, and it automatically re-aligns to the tiles you already placed. There is no upper limit on total project size.

**Q: Does the laser work in daylight?**
A: Yes. The 200mW 520nm green laser is visible in normal indoor lighting conditions — kitchens, bathrooms, and living rooms. Direct sunlight on the projection surface will reduce visibility. For outdoor use (patios, pool decks), working in shade or during overcast conditions is recommended.

**Q: What is the difference between Flagship and Pro?**
A: The only difference is power. Flagship is corded (USB-C) — you need a wall outlet or a USB-C power bank. Pro adds two swappable battery packs for 1.5–2 hours of cordless operation per pack, plus automatic switchover between cord and battery. The laser, camera, vision system, and app are identical.

**Q: Can I use the app without the laser?**
A: Absolutely. The app is a complete tile layout tool on its own. It calculates your layout, optimizes cut placement, provides exact measurements for every cut, and generates material lists. Many users will get significant value from the app alone. The laser adds the ability to project that layout onto the real surface at 1:1 scale.

**Q: What types of tile patterns are supported?**
A: Square, rectangle, hexagon, herringbone, and 17 mosaic sub-tile shapes including arabesque, fish scale, penny round, octagon-and-dot, and dragon scale. 234 built-in presets cover every common tile from 2-inch hex mosaics to 8x48 wood-look planks. Custom tile entry supports any size and shape.

**Q: Will the laser work with patterns other than tile?**
A: At launch, the laser is optimized for tile installation. Our patents are domain-general — they cover any repeating material pattern on a surface. Hardwood, LVP, pavers, and shingles are on the roadmap (see stretch goals). The same hardware handles all of them with software updates.

**Q: What if I bump the laser during installation?**
A: The built-in 6-axis IMU detects any displacement. The camera immediately scans for existing tile edges and re-locks the projection. A green/yellow/red confidence indicator in the app shows alignment status. In testing, recovery takes 2–3 seconds. You do not need to manually recalibrate.

**Q: Do I need WiFi or internet access to use the laser?**
A: The laser connects to the app via a direct WiFi connection (the laser creates its own hotspot). You do not need internet access on the job site. The AI scanning features (tile label reading, room photo scanning) require internet, but those are done during the planning phase, not during installation.

**Q: Can I use this for walls and floors?**
A: Yes. The laser projects onto any flat surface — walls, floors, countertops, shower surrounds. The vision alignment system works the same way regardless of surface orientation. For floors, a tripod or elevated surface positions the laser to project downward at an angle.

## Risks and Challenges

We believe in being straightforward about what could go wrong.

**Manufacturing timeline.** We are transitioning from hand-built prototypes to a production run. The electronics are all commercially available components — no custom silicon, no exotic parts. But assembly, quality control, and testing at volume is different from building one unit on a workbench. We have identified contract manufacturing partners and have detailed assembly documentation, but first-run production always carries schedule risk. Our timeline includes buffer, but delays of 4–8 weeks are possible.

**Supply chain.** The galvanometer scanner sets are sourced from specialized manufacturers, primarily in China. Lead times are typically 2–4 weeks but can stretch during peak demand. We are mitigating this by placing component orders early and maintaining relationships with multiple suppliers. All other components (ESP32, camera modules, laser diodes, enclosures) are broadly available from multiple sources.

**Laser safety compliance.** The 200mW Class 3B laser requires proper safety labeling, documentation, and included safety glasses. We are working with laser safety consultants to ensure full compliance with FDA/CDRH regulations (21 CFR 1040) before any units ship. This is a well-understood regulatory path — thousands of Class 3B laser products are sold commercially — but the paperwork takes time.

**Software maturity.** The app is functional and actively used, but software is never done. We will continue improving the AI scanning accuracy, adding tile presets to the barcode database, and refining the laser communication protocol throughout the campaign and production period. Backers should expect regular app updates and occasional firmware updates after receiving their laser.

**Vision alignment in edge cases.** The self-correcting vision system works well in controlled testing with standard tile on flat surfaces. Highly reflective tile, very dark surfaces, or unusual lighting conditions may affect camera-based edge detection. We are actively expanding the range of conditions the system handles, and firmware updates will continue to improve performance after launch.

**This is a real product, not a render.** The app exists. The laser prototype works. The patents are filed. We are not asking you to fund R&D — we are asking you to fund production. That said, hardware is hard, and we are a small team. We will communicate openly about progress, setbacks, and timelines throughout the campaign and fulfillment process.
